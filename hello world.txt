    <h1>History of Video Games</h1>
      <p>Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.). <em>Originally</em> designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning, generating <strong>billions of dollars</strong> each year. <br /><br />"All your base are belong to us"</p>
    <h2>The Beginning (1950s)</h2>
      <p>In 1952, British professor A.S. Douglas created <em>OXO</em>, also known as noughts and crosses or a tic-tac-toe.<br/><br />In 1958, William Higinbotham created Tennis for Two on a large analog computer.<br /><br />In 1962, Steve Russell invented Spacewar!, a computer-based space combat video game.</p>
    <h3>Video Games at Home (1960-1983)</h3>
      <p>Developers at Sanders Associates, Inc. in 1967 invented a prototype mutliplayer, multi-program video game system that could be played on television. They were led by Ralph Baer.<br /><br />In the next couple years after 1972, the Odyssey console's popularity began to fizzle out and die.</p>
      <p>In 1978, the Space Invaders arcade game was released.</p>
